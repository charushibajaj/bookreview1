REVIEW: The Help by Kathryn Stockett is a powerful and thought-provoking novel. Set in 1962 Mississippi, it follows three extraordinary women: Skeeter, a recent college graduate; Aibileen, a black maid; and Minny, Aibileen's sassy best friend. Together, they embark on a clandestine project that challenges the racial norms of their time. The book vividly portrays the racism prevalent in America during that era, giving voice to those often unheard.

REVIEW: City of Bones by Cassandra Clare is a gripping urban fantasy novel that captures readers with its supernatural world and compelling characters. With intricate world-building and complex mythology, it transports readers to a mesmerizing realm where demons and Shadowhunters coexist. The story begins when fifteen-year-old Clary Fray witnesses a murder committed by three teenagers covered in strange tattoos and wielding bizarre weapons. As she delves deeper into their world, she discovers secrets, danger, and unexpected alliances. If you enjoy urban fantasy and supernatural adventures, this book is definitely worth a read!

REVIEW: Cinder by Marissa Meyer is a captivating blend of science fiction, fantasy, and romance. The story unfolds in New Beijing, where humans, androids, and a deadly plague collide, and Earth's fate rests on Cinder's shoulders.This unique retelling features a complex story arc beyond the classic Cinderella tale. Cinder, a cyborg mechanic, becomes entangled with Prince Kai while dealing with plague research. A favorite for fairy-tale lovers.

REVIEW: Verity by Colleen Hoover is a mind-bending psychological thriller that has garnered significant attention. The novel revolves around Lowen Ashleigh, a struggling writer who accepts a job offer from Jeremy Crawford, the husband of bestselling author Verity Crawford. Jeremy hires Lowen to complete the remaining books in Verity's successful series, as Verity is unable to finish them due to an injury. However, when Lowen delves into Verity's notes and outlines, she discovers an unfinished autobiography that reveals shocking secrets, including what really happened the day Verity's daughter died. The book keeps readers guessing and questioning everything they thought they knew12. Some readers have found it bold and intriguing, while others have described it as a fever dream with an original plot3. Overall, it's a book that leaves a lasting impact and sparks intense discussions among readers4. If you enjoy psychological thrillers with complex characters and unexpected twists, “Verity” might be a great choice for you!

REVIEW: Fangirl by Rainbow Rowell is a heartfelt coming-of-age tale that delves into the world of fanfiction and the challenges of growing up. The story revolves around Cath, a devoted Simon Snow fan who finds solace in fandom. As she navigates college life, she grapples with family dynamics, friendships, and first love. The book captures the essence of being a fangirl and offers relatable insights into relationships.
